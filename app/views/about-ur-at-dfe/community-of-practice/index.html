Note that this Slack is only open to DfE staff, current contractors, and people working in agencies and bodies within the department. You will need an education.gov.uk email address to join. MOst user researchers will be given Slack access when you join DfE. ## Community meetups If you are a user researcher (civil servant and contractor) at DfE, you are invited and encouraged to attend our community meetups: regular sessions where user researchers come together in person, or remotely over MS Teams. We have monthly national meetups, run remotely, and each DfE office location with digital staff has a local meetup, run as a hybrid session: people are encouraged to attend in-person, but can also join over Teams. Everyone is welcome to get involved, to help steer the future of our national meetups. ## Community initiatives and interest groups Whatever your interest in user research, there are probably other researchers in our community who share it. We have lots of voluntary working groups who work together on community initiatives, as well as interest groups who come come together to talk about a shared interest, share experiences, and sometimes design best practice. We invite and encourage every user researcher to get involved with a working group or interest group. This includes contractors: we really value the expertise and experiences you bring from other organisations you've worked in. For civil servant user researchers, actively contributing to one of these groups could form your profession objective. Our current groups are listed below, with the people to contact if you would like to get involved. If you don't see a group formed around your interest, then why not start it yourself? Use our community Slack channels to find people who share the interest, get together, and let us know if you create a working group so we can add it to this page. ### Working groups As well as to allow people with a shared interest to come together, our working groups have been set up to create community-led improvements to our user research practice at DfE. They each have a defined goal, and will create outputs: for example, our Ethics working group was set up to deliver guidance for ethical user research, and help develop our user research standards. Some of these groups are focused on developing UR practice and capability across the whole of DfE. Others have been formed to experiment around a topic in a single programme or portfolio, in order to identify changes that might in the future be rolled out across DfE.
