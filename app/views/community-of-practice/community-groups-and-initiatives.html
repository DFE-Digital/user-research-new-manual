It is open for any user researcher (civil servant or contractor) to join It was born in the Vulnerable Children and Families portfolio in 2020 and grew into a truly collaborative cross-DfE working group. Everything we do aims to ensure we stick to the DfE UR Principles - especially “We do high quality, safe and ethical research” and “We learn as a community and by ourselves” We aim to do this by - Helping User Researchers make the best/optimal ethical decisions for their context - Ensuring User Researchers feel confident their research methods are ethically sound We have produced, and are continuing to produce, ethics and safeguarding guidance for DfE user researchers, and other guidance on topics like inclusive research, using incentives, and others. We also offer objective, non-judgemental, ad-hoc peer advice to any DfE user researcher wrangling with tricky ethical issues in their research. You can ask questions in the #ethicsadvisorygroup channel in DfE Slack. We are always thinking of new ways to help User Researchers in our community upload high ethical standards in their practice. We are now focused on: - improving URs' access to the right support - sharing and learning practices across government - making sure we understand user researchers' needs - iterating the guidance and supporting the development of DfE UR standards If you have a passion for research ethics, want to get to know other URs in the DfE, learn new things or help grow the UR community, you can join ethics club by contacting Will Finn in DfE Slack. If you're from another government department, and are interested in what we're doing in Ethics Club, contact Will in cross-government Slack. ## Community initiatives and interest groups Whatever your interest in user research, there are probably other researchers in our community who share it. We have lots of voluntary working groups who work together on community initiatives, as well as interest groups who come come together to talk about a shared interest, share experiences, and sometimes design best practice. We invite and encourage every user researcher to get involved with a working group or interest group. This includes contractors: we really value the expertise and experiences you bring from other organisations you've worked in. For civil servant user researchers, actively contributing to one of these groups could form your profession objective. Our current groups are listed below, with the people to contact if you would like to get involved. If you don't see a group formed around your interest, then why not start it yourself? Use our community Slack channels to find people who share the interest, get together, and let us know if you create a working group so we can add it to this page. ### Working groups As well as to allow people with a shared interest to come together, our working groups have been set up to create community-led improvements to our user research practice at DfE. They each have a defined goal, and will create outputs: for example, our Ethics working group was set up to deliver guidance for ethical user research, and help develop our user research standards. Some of these groups are focused on developing UR practice and capability across the whole of DfE. Others have been formed to experiment around a topic in a single programme or portfolio, in order to identify changes that might in the future be rolled out across DfE.
