    • {% endblock %} {# --------- Main page content -------------------------------------------------- #} {% block content %} {% markdown %} You can offer monetary incentives to most DfE user groups. There are some groups that you must not offer them to, and some where you need to take additional care in your decision to offer them. If you do offer monetary incentives, it is the responsibility of the individual participant to check that they can accept it under any rules or policy in their own organisation or profession. ## Groups you must not offer monetary incentives to You must never offer a monetary incentive to: - anybody currently employed by DfE - anybody currently working in or on behalf of DfE as a contractor or supplier - family members, friends or other people that you have a pre-existing close personal relationship with - prisoners or other people in detention including young offenders - asylum seekers, undocumented migrants, or immigrants with no recourse to public funds - any person with a professional gatekeeping responsibility over a child who is taking part in your research (e.g. social worker, care home worker, etc) - any child or young person taking part in research during school hours in a state-funded education setting - pre-school or primary age children - You may offer an incentive to the child's parent or guardian (unless the guardian is acting in a professional capacity, e.g. a care home worker). ## Offering monetary incentives to teachers, school staff and other public servants You can offer monetary incentives to public servants and other people employed and paid directly or indirectly by public money. You must take additional care to show that monetary incentives are required and appropriate, being particularly aware of any current major news stories or controversies involving your user groups and DfE or the wider government. Get advice from your lead user researcher or policy colleagues if you need to. These user groups include: - teachers and other staff currently employed in state funded education settings - civil servants outside of DfE, and contractors and suppliers currently working for other government departments and agencies - local authority employees, and contractors and suppliers currently working for local authorities - social workers - doctors and other care professionals Alternative options you can take to offering monetary incentives to individuals in these user groups include offering the incentive to their organisation (e.g. to a school to buy library books) or offering a charity donation. ## People outside of the UK You can offer monetary incentives to non-UK based user research participants. Check with research operations that our incentives suppliers can arrange payment in the countries you are planning to recruit from. {% endmarkdown%} {% endblock %} {# --------- Related content (right-hand column) -------------------------------- #} {% block related %} {% markdown %}
