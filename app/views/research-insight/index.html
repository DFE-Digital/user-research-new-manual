Our service areas and users

Across Department for Education, we have a wide and diverse set of users. Around half the English population are users of our services, or are end-beneficiaries of our policies.

We broadly organise users into DfE's three priority outcome areas (DfE Intranet), which guide the work we do as a department: Skills, Schools and Families.

  • Skills

    In Skills, our service areas include Apprenticeships, National Careers Service, T Levels and other policy and services around the skills that support people to work.

    Our users include training providers, further education providers, careers advisors, employers, adult learners, young people moving towards the end of formal education and their families, and operational staff in DfE and our partners.

    Find existing research about Skills service users (DfE SharePoint)

  • Schools (and teachers)

    DfE delivers a wide range of services for schools and teachers, including services to recruit and upskill the teaching workforce, services to support the efficient running of schools and academy trusts, funding sevrices, and services to collect educational data and present that data to the public.

    Users include teachers and prospective teachers, senior teachers and school leaders, school business professionals and other people involved in the administration and leadership of schools and trusts. The end beneficiaries of the policies that inform our services are students, and so sometimes we will research with chilfren and their families.
