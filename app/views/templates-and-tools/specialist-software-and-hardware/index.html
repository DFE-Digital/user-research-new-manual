! Warning You must not install any software or use any web service that collects personal data, unless it has gained DfE data protection and security assurance
There are a range of tools you will need when conducting user research. Some of these are available to you via centrally managed licenses, while others can be purchased by your team. ## Laptops and access to DfE systems If you are a civil servant DfE user researcher, our standard laptops aren't always powerful enough for tasks like video editing, or some specialist software. Instead, you can order a more powerful laptop. Go to the Service Portal and search for 'Power device'. There are certain DfE internal tools and services (including some that you might need for desk research, like the Department's Research and Evidence Directory, and some data dashboards) that can only be accessed by civil servants, or by contractors with a DfE laptop. If you are a contractor who needs to access these services, then you can ask a civil servant in your team, or you can ask your delivery manager to arrange for you to be loaned a DfE laptop. ## Specialist software and online tools There are some specialist user research software and online tools that are available to all user researchers. The current list is on the Digital Tools Support team website. You can contact that team for more support in the Digital Tools Support Slack channel There may be a cost to your team for these tools, so speak to your delivery manager first. You can also speak to the Digital Tools Support team about buying licenses for other software or online services. They can tell you what you and your delivery manager will need (e.g. a business case and budget approval). If the software or service doesn't already have data and security assurance, they will arrange this. This process can sometimes take a few weeks, so leave plenty of time to do this. You can also contact them if a tool you need to use is not available, or if you already have access to digital tools and have questions. ### Artificial intelligence/large language models If you are planning to use any AI tools like ChatGPT, Google Bard, or software or plugins built on top of them, then as well as ensuring, you should follow the departmental guidance (DfE intranet). You should not do any analysis of personal data in these tools or put any confidential content into them. The Department is working with AI suppliers on future guidelines and private tools. ## Other tools We don't currently have any centrally available tools like video cameras, dictaphones, etc. We're working on this (see our profession roadmap). For now, if you need these, you can buy them with your team or programme area's budget. Your delivery manager can help you with this. {% endmarkdown%} {% endblock %}
